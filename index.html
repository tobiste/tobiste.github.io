I am a postdoctoral fellow at Lakehead University (Canada).

I work in structural geology and tectonics.

My main research interest is the relationship between the deformation of rocks and the plate tectonic motion. To analyze these patterns from the micro-scale (thin sections) to large scale (tectonic plates), I use techniques from structural geology, metamorphic petrology, crystallographic preferred orientation, geophysics, geochemistry, geo-thermochronology, and provenance analysis. My research is based on field work, lab work, and computer modelling. My main research subjects are:

Stress and strain field analysis: The spherical shape of the Earth challenges statistical approaches to analyse existing stress fields or predict stress fields. Despite these obstacles, I develop methods and tools to predict the orientation of crustal stresses. The only assumption: plate tectonics. More…

Plate motion: Euler poles of plate motion can move? Yes, but the cause can be geometry only. Learn more…

Tectonic reconstruction: Past plate motion can be reconstructed based on the magnetic stripes of oceanic crust or paleomagnetic information. But what to do when oceanic crust is missing and paleomagnetiu data is limited? Learn how to use the spherical strain pattern of continental crust to reconstruct ancient plate motion here

Tectonic deformation, fluid flow, and mineral deposits: How the tectonic setting, type, and intensity of deformation control fluid flow and concentration of metals: Check it out

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen-Nuremberg (Germany) and University of Calgary (Canada). I graduated with a PhD from TU Bergakademie Freiberg (Germany) in 2019.
